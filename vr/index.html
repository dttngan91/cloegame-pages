Our Story

Since 2015, the XR division at CloeGames has been dedicated to exploring cutting-edge VR and AR technologies.

The lab specializes in diverse projects, ranging from immersive games and realistic medical simulations to creative brand marketing events.

By staying ahead of emerging trends and working on-demand, the division delivers innovative solutions tailored to client needs, showcasing expertise in both entertainment and practical applications of XR technologies.

Home Aquarium

This mixed reality (MR) demo showcases a virtual fish tank aquarium designed as a decorative and relaxation feature for the Apple Vision Pro. The experience blends immersive visuals with interactive elements, creating a calming and aesthetically pleasing environment. Perfect for enhancing living spaces or work environments, this demo highlights the potential of MR in delivering innovative and tranquil applications for everyday use.

Virtual Motorbike Fixing Simulator

This VR project, developed for a Japanese company over three months, leverages the Meta Quest platform to create an immersive training environment for mechanics. The simulation enables hands-on practice for repairing motorbikes, offering a safe and effective way to develop and refine technical skills. Emphasizing realism and precision, the project serves as an advanced tool for vocational training and skill enhancement.

Hip Replacement Surgery Simulation

This innovative VR project offers a dual-platform experience for medical training in hip replacement surgery. Developed with versions for Google Cardboard VR and Leap Motion controls, it provides an immersive, hands-on learning tool for medical students and professionals. The Google Cardboard VR version offers accessibility and portability, while the Leap Motion control version enhances interactivity and precision, simulating the intricate hand movements required in surgery. This project is designed to improve surgical training by combining realism with cutting-edge technology.

Virtual Pet Game

This Explore, developed as part of an MRES research project at Bournemouth, UK (2015), showcased innovative VR pet game development using Oculus DK2. The focus was on comparing the performance and user experience of VR hand interactions via Kinect and Leap Motion controllers. By evaluating their capabilities in a VR environment, the project provided valuable insights into the state of VR interaction technology at the time, highlighting usability challenges and opportunities for immersive gaming experiences.
